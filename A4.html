Rayleigh Jean's criterion¶

According to Rayleigh-Jeans, em waves confined to a cavity at temperature $T$ with walls as perfect reflectors consist of standing waves filling a certain volume V0 of the cavity, with density of states given by

Density of States $$ G(\nu)d\nu = \frac{8\pi \nu^2 d\nu}{c^3}V_0 \implies G(\nu)d\left(\frac{\nu}{\nu_0}\right) = \frac{\pi}{\nu_0} \left(\frac{\nu }{\nu_0}\right)^2d\left(\frac{\nu}{\nu_0}\right) $$ where $V_0 = l_0^3$ and $\nu_0 := c/(2l_0)$, if $x := \nu/\nu_0$

$$ G(\nu)dx = \frac{\pi}{\nu_0} x^2dx $$

Therefore, Density of states($G^{*}$, which is unfortunately dimensional) as a function of dimensionless variable $x$, $$ G^{*}(x) = \frac{\pi}{\nu_0} x^2 \implies \boxed{G^{*}(x) \nu_0 = \pi x^2} $$

since E.M. waves consists of two degrees of freedom, each of the standing waves shall have energy $\bar{\epsilon} = kT$ according to the Law of Equipartition of energy. Thus, the energy density for a particular mode will be $$u(\nu)d\nu = \bar{\epsilon}\dfrac{G(\nu)d\nu}{V_0} = \dfrac{8\nu^2kT}{c^3} d\nu$$ Energy density(function of frequency) $u(\nu)d\nu$ $$u(\nu) = \dfrac{8\nu^2kT}{c^3}$$

def rj_u(nu,t):
    es = k*t
    ls = h*C/es
    x = h*nu/es 
    f = x**2
    u = 8*np.pi*h/ls**3*f
    return(x,f,u)

def planck_u(nu,t):
    es = k*t
    ls = h*C/es
    xn,fn = lambda nu_: h*nu_ /es, lambda x:  x**3/(np.exp(x)-1)
    x = xn(nu)
    f = fn(x)
    u = 8*np.pi*h/ls**3*f
    return(x,f,u)
    
rj_u = np.vectorize(rj_u)
planck_u = np.vectorize(planck_u)
